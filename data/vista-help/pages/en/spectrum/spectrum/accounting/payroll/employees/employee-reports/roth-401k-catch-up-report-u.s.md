# Roth 401K Catch-Up Report (U.S.)

Use the Roth 401K Catch-Up Report to determine which employees are subject to the catch-up contribution rule that requires use of a post-tax Roth.
Find this report at Payroll > Reports > Roth 401K Catch-Up Report.
This report applies only if both are true:

- Your company's workplace retirement plan allows catch-up contributions.

- Your prior year payroll data exists in Spectrum. If this is your first year in the application, you will need to rely on the W-2 data in your previous system to determine which employees hit the earnings threshold.

This report identifies employees who satisfy both these eligibility criteria and must therefore use a post-tax Roth IRA as their vehicle for catch-up contributions:

- The employee will reach age 50 or older in the current year.Important:If a birth date is missing from the employee's record
Any employee that meets the income threshold and whose record in the Employee Personal Information screen does not have a birth date will be included on the report. Of these, you will need to either enter a birth date and run the report again, or use some other way to determine which employees are ages 50 and above.

- The employee's prior-year Box 3 Social Security wages are equal to or exceed the federal threshold, which the IRS adjusts annually.Important:If an employee is new to your organization
Any new employee for which the organization doesn't have a prior year's W-2 to review is treated as if their wages were under the threshold. This applies even when it is expected that the employee's wages will surpass the threshold in the current year.

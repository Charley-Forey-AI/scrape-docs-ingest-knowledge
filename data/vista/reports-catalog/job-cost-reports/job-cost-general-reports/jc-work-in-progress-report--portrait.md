# JC Work in Progress Report – Portrait

You can use the JC Work in Progress Report – Portrait
 report by selecting Job Cost > Reports > JC Work in Progress Report –
 Portrait.
This report is identical to JC Work in Progress Report - Landscape, except that it prints in Portrait. Using the percentage of completion method, the primary purpose of this report is to calculate earned revenue and over/under billings by Contract. Earned Revenue is the % Complete multiplied by the Current Contract Amount, where % Complete is the ratio of Actual Cost to Estimated Cost of Completion. Estimated Cost of Completion is either the total projected or current estimated cost by contract item - e.g., item 1 projected cost = $500, item 2 projected cost = $0 projected cost, $700 of current estimated cost, Total contract Estimated Cost @ Complete = $1,200. Following standard accounting practices, Earned Revenue will be adjusted as follows if the report projects a loss, or in other words, the contract amount minus estimated cost @ completion is less than 0. Contract Amount + Actual Cost - Estimated Cost @ Completion. In order to make reconciling to GL easier, the report groups by job cost department (assigned to the contract item) and allows limiting to closed contracts in the current year via the beg/end closed month parameters.

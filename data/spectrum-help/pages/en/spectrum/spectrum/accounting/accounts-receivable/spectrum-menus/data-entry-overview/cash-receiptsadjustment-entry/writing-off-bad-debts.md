# Writing Off Bad Debts

Once you have established that a debt will not be paid
 back, you will want to write off this bad debt.
To do this, you must first set up a transaction account
 specifically for writing off bad debts. Once the appropriate transaction account has been
 instituted, you can proceed with writing off a bad debt.Note: In most instances, you do not want to issue a credit memo for bad
 debts.

1. Open the Cash Receipts/Adjustment Entry screen.

1. At the Customer code field, enter the customer code whose bad debt you are
 writing off.

1. At the Transaction type field, enter the transaction code you use for bad
 debts.

1. At the Reference # field, the software will assign the cash
 receipt/adjustment a reference number. Press Enter to accept this default.
 Otherwise, assign the cash receipt/adjustment a reference number of your choosing and
 press Enter.

1. In the Transaction section, enter the date, and amount you are writing off,
 and then press Enter.

1. The Cash
 Receipts/Adjustments Entry Selections window displays. Review the
 selections, make any necessary adjustments and click OK.

1. At the Discount field in the line transaction portion of the screen, enter
 0.

1. At the Paid/adjusted field, enter the bad debt amount you are writing off,
 and then press Enter. This
 amount should coincide with the amount entered in the Amount
 field in the transaction portion of the screen. Click OK.

1. Click the Listing button, and then click the
 Preview button. Verify
 that the bad debt cash receipt/adjustment and invoice balance(s) you entered are
 correct on the sample report.

1. Once you have completed reviewing the report, click
 OK to return to the CashReceipts/AdjustmentsEntry screen.


1. Click Update, and then click
 Preview to review the sample report.

1. Click Continue to complete the
 update.

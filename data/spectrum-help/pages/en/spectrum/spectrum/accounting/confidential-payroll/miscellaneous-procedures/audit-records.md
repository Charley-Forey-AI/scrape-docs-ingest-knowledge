# Audit Records

Printed reports serve as important audit trail documents in a computerized accounting system because detail transactions are sometimes removed during period end processing to conserve disk space. Even when disk space is a secondary issue, detail records may be removed to speed operations or to reduce historical display. These reports may also be referred to in case of hardware problems. It is therefore important to maintain these reports in an orderly fashion and on a regular basis.
As a general rule when using the Spectrum Construction Management software, whenever a selection provides for a report followed by an option to update, this will be an important report to retain on file. In these cases, the final report printed prior to update is the important document to keep. In addition to these routine reports, there are certain "period end" reports that should also be printed and filed as a permanent record of the company.
The following is a suggested list of reports that should be maintained from Payroll. This is a minimum listing for accounting purposes; companies are encouraged to retain other Payroll reports as well, depending upon individual needs.
Important: These reports should be retained as permanent
 audit records of the company.

## Payroll Cycle Reports (printed in Payroll module)

- Time Card Edit List

- State Wage and Tax Listing

- Pre-Check Register

- Check Register

- Job Distribution Report

- Labor Distribution Report

- Deduction Report

- Add-on Report

- Payroll G/L Summary Report

## Period End Reports (printed in Confidential Payroll module)

- Quarterly Federal Tax Report

- Unemployment Tax Report

- Subject-to-Tax Report

- State Disability Insurance Report

- Worker's Comp Rate Per $100 Report

- Worker's Comp Rate Per Hour Report

- Deduction History Report

- Add-on History Report

- Union Report

- Employment Utilization Report

- Print W-2 Forms

- Print W-3 Forms

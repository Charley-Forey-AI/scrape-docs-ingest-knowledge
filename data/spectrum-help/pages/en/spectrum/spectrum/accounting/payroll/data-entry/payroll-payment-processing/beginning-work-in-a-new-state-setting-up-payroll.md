# Beginning Work in a New State: Setting Up Payroll

Use this procedure when setting up payroll if you have employees conducting work in a new state.

1. On the Site Map
 screen, click Payroll > Maintenance > Tax tables to display the Tax Table
 Maintenance screen. Complete this screen. Be sure to select the
 Resident credit check
 box if the non-resident tax amount should be deducted from the resident tax amount;
 otherwise, leave this checkbox clear.

1. Click OK.

1. To setup temporary exemptions and prevent duplicate state taxation, on the
 Site Map screen, click
 Payroll > Maintenance > Income Tax to open the Income Tax
 Maintenance screen. Click the Temp Exempt button and enter the
 state information, including which state(s) are receiving the tax. Click OK.Note: Temporary exemptions are critical to assigning the correct tax to the employee.
 The temporary exemption defines the effects in the work state and residence
 state.

1. On the Site Map
 screen, click Job Cost > Maintenance > Job to open the Job File
 Maintenance screen.

1. Enter the job number and when the Address and Personnel information displays,
 click the Properties button
 and then the Tax Codes
 button. Enter the Work
 state, Work county, and Work
 local information, or select from the drop-down list. Click OK.
Important: If your state does NOT charge county and local taxes,
 leave these fields blank; otherwise, you will overpay your taxes.

1. Click each of the buttons on the right-hand side of the Jobs screen and enter the appropriate information. Click OK to save your entries.

1. On the Site Map
 screen, click Payroll > Maintenance > Worker's > Compensation to display the Worker's
 Compensation Code Maintenance screen. Complete this screen and click
 OK.

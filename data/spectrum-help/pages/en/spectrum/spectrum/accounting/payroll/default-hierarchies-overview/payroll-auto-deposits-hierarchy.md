# Payroll Auto Deposits Hierarchy

Spectrum allows up to five different accounts to deposit an
 employee's paycheck.
You can allocate money to these accounts using a percentage or fixed rate methodology.

- If the allocation method is based on percent, Spectrum requires that the sum of all percentages be 100.

- If the allocation method is based on a fixed amount, Spectrum uses the following hierarchy to allocate the employee's paycheck:

This shows the priority of distributing the amount of the check. If Fixed amount was specified in the Allocation method group box, then the set dollar amounts will be deposited into the accounts in order of priority until the amounts can no longer be filled. If all priorities are fulfilled and there is money remaining, it will be deposited into the first account specified.
For example, if the priority 1 account has a rate of $1,000.00, the priority 2 account has a rate of $200.00, and the priority 3 account has a priority of $50.00, and the net check amount is $1,100.00, then $1,000.00 will be deposited into the priority 1 account, and $100.00 will be deposited into the priority 2 account. Using the same priority accounts, if the net check amount is $2,000.00, all three priority accounts will have the fixed amount deposited, and the remaining $750.00 will be deposited into the priority 1 account, so the total deposit to the priority 1 account will be $1,750.00.

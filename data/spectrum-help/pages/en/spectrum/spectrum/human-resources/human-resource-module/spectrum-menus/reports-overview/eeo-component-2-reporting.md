# EEO Component 2 Reporting

Federal EEO Component 2 reporting is required to be submitted by qualifying employers. Spectrum's enhanced EEO report includes salary range information as well as a count of employees in each EEO category.
 This document outlines the procedures to prepare an accurate Spectrum EEO report.Because Component 2 salary ranges are based on W2 wages (Box 1), the Spectrum EEO report uses the W2 Build (in Payroll > Period End > W2 Processing) to categorize employees into the proper salary range.
The report prints EEO detail for each establishment or location. Within each establishment, there is a section reporting the number of employees, and a second section with the number of hours worked. Finally, the total numbers of employees and hours are reported for all locations in a consolidated section.
Please refer to the United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) website for more details on Component 2 requirements, and for EEOC contact and login information to file component 2 online.
A sample image of the EEO-1 Component 2 report. Depicted salary ranges are subject to updates as issued by the EEOC.

1. Validate W2 data:

1. Navigate to Payroll > Period End > W2 Processing.

1. Click the Listing button at the top of the screen.

1. Run in summary to check accuracy of totals, or run in detail to check accuracy of specific employee W2 amounts.Tip: The only information that is used for the Component 2 salary range is Box 1. There is no need to correct or change any other Box values.

1. If the Box 1 data for the given year has been over-written for any reason, it will be necessary to re-build the data for that year.

1. Run the EEO Employment Report for the given year:

1. Navigate to Human Resources > Reports > EEO Employment.

1. Choose Report type: Employees.

1. Enter the year.

1. Print the report for the given year and use it to fill out the online EEO survey form.

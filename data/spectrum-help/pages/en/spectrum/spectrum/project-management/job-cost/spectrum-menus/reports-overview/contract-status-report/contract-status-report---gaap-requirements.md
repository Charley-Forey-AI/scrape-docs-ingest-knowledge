# Contract Status Report - GAAP Requirements

If a job is in a loss position, GAAP requires that the loss
 be recognized in the first period when it is determined that a loss will be incurred.
 Therefore, if a job has a negative gross profit amount projected at completion, a second line
 that includes the provision for loss amount will display on the JTD contract status with YTD, JTD contract status with MTD, and JTD contract status formats of the Contract
 Status Report.
Furthermore, if there is more than one job in a loss position, a recap
 section will list these jobs separately from the other jobs, and will include the job
 number, job name, and provision for loss amount.
Provision should be made for the entire loss on a contract in the period
 when the current estimates of total contract costs indicate a loss. The provision for loss
 amount is typically entered as a reversing journal entry, with the full job-to-date amount
 booked for each period the adjustment is made.
Important: Any provision for loss amounts are accounted for
 in the income statement as an additional cost rather than as a reduction of contract
 revenues. Provision for Loss = Gross Profit (Projected at Completion) less Gross Profit
 (Job-to-Date).

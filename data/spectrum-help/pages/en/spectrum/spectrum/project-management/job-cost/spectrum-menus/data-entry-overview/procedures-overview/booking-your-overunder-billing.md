# Booking Your Over/Under Billing

At the end of each period, the company determines the
 amounts of over and under billings using a work-in-progress schedule. In order for the report
 to be accurate, all costs and billings must be posted prior to creating the Contract Status Report.
G.A.A.P. (Generally Accepted Accounting Principals) suggests special
 handling for work on jobs in progress across several accounting periods. Calculations
 and journal entries are performed insuring the correct amount of income is recorded,
 regardless of what is billed. It is recommended that you run the Contract Status Report monthly,
 although the IRS only requires annual notification of your over and under contract
 billings. The Contract Status Report may be printed with or without projected costs. If
 projected costs are not included, Spectrum uses the current estimated costs in its
 report calculations. In order to determine which format best meets your company's needs,
 it is recommended that you thoroughly understand how Spectrum calculates projected and
 estimated costs.
Complete the following steps to accurately compile your company's
 Contract Status Report:


1. Enter and update all costs and billings into Spectrum.

1. On the Site Map screen, click Job Cost  >  Reports  >  Contract Status to open the Contract
 Status Report screen.

1. Complete the Selections portion of the
 screen.

1. Complete the Report format portion of the screen
 if you are using a non-Crystal report format.

1. Complete the Report period portion of the
 screen.

1. Click the Preview or Print button to view a copy of the
 Contract Status
 Report.

1. Enter the over and under billing amounts that appear
 in the Balance Sheet column
 of the report in the following G/L accounts: Costs in Excess of Billings,
 Billings in Excess of
 Costs, and Net Over /
 Under Billings. To book the billing over and under costs, two journal
 entries are typically required at the period end.

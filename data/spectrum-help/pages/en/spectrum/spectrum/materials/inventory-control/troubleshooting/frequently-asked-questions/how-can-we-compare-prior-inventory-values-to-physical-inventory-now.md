# How can we compare prior inventory values to physical inventory
 now?

Business Problem: We do a physical inventory twice per year. It takes about two weeks into the next period before we're done. During this time, how can we determine what the inventory value was at the end of the year?
Solution: The Items-On-Hand Summary Valuation Report provides the cost basis value of inventory stock-on-hand as of a specified date. This report can be used to substantiate the value of the company's inventory asset and is often printed on a monthly or quarterly basis for inclusion in the company's accounting records. The Standard Cost format, which prints one line per item and uses standard cost rather than "actual" cost, might be used for insurance replacement-cost planning. This report should be retained as a permanent audit record of the company.
